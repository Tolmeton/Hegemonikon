# Handoff: Peras Output Refactoring (1-Pass Compression Fix)

## 📌 Situation
The LLM's tendency to compress complex Peras SKILL logic into 1-line summaries circumvented previous fixes. Even with constraints in place, a single `<:process:>` tag caused the model to revert to 1-pass summarization, effectively hiding the internal CoT (Chain of Thought).

## 🔭 Background
We updated `_peras-output-templates` and individual Peras SKILLs (`t`, `m`, `k`, `d`, `o`, `c`, `ax`) to use TYPOS format. However, the placeholder `<:process: {詳細...} :>` was too broad, causing the LLM's completion bias to collapse the entire CoT into a brief summary. The user explicitly demanded maximum depth without compression, utilizing the 65k token output limit.

## 🧠 Assessment (Doxa)
- **Problem**: A single placeholder tag acts as an inadequate structural scaffold for multi-phase processes. 
- **Solution**: We explicitly hardcoded the phase tags (`<:Phase_0:>` through `<:Phase_4_and_beyond:>`) inside the `<:process:>` block for every constituent verb in all 7 Peras SKILL files. This leverages the LLM's tendency to fill out explicitly requested fields, mechanically precluding 1-pass summarization.
- **Result**: All Peras SKILL files were successfully updated. Testing with `/t+` confirmed the structural scaffolding enforces full depth generation.
